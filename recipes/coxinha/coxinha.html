Coxinha

Description

Coxinha is a popular Brazilian snack made of dough filled with shredded chicken and often cream cheese. The dough is shaped like a teardrop, resembling a chicken drumstick. After being filled, the coxinha is coated in breadcrumbs and deep-fried until golden brown. It's known for its crispy exterior and savory filling, making it a favorite at parties, street food stalls, and casual gatherings in Brazil.

Ingredients:

  • Flour;
  • Butter;
  • Salt;
  • Shredded chicken;
  • Onion;
  • Garlic;
  • Parsley or cilantro;
  • Eggs;
  • Breadcrumbs.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a skillet, sauté the chopped onion (and garlic, if using) until translucent. Add the shredded chicken, salt, pepper, and any herbs. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. If desired, stir in cream cheese and fresh herbs. Remove from heat and let cool;
  2. Make the Dough: In a saucepan, combine chicken broth, butter, and salt. Bring to a boil. Gradually add the flour, stirring continuously until a dough forms. Cook for a few minutes until the dough pulls away from the sides of the pan. Let it cool slightly;
  3. Shape the Coxinha: Once the dough is cool enough to handle, take a small piece (about the size of a golf ball) and flatten it in your palm. Place a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center, then fold the dough over the filling and shape it into a teardrop or drumstick shape. Ensure the filling is completely sealed inside;
  4. Coat the Coxinhas: Dip each shaped coxinha into the beaten eggs, then roll in breadcrumbs until fully coated;
  5. Fry the Coxinhas: Heat oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C). Fry the coxinhas in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels;
  6. Serve: Enjoy your coxinhas hot, with your favorite dipping sauce!

Feel free to customize the filling or try baking them for a lighter option. Enjoy!
