Tapioca

Description:

Tapioca is a starchy substance derived from the cassava root, a tuber native to South America. It’s commonly processed into pearls, flour, or flakes and is a popular ingredient in various dishes. In Brazilian cuisine, tapioca is often used to make flatbreads or crepes, which can be filled with sweet or savory fillings. It’s gluten-free and has a chewy texture when cooked, making it a versatile ingredient in both desserts (like tapioca pudding) and savory snacks.

Ingredients:

  • Tapioca flour;
  • Water;
  • Salt;
  • Fillings (that could be savory or sweet).

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Tapioca Flour: If using dry tapioca flour, place it in a bowl. Add a pinch of salt if desired. Slowly add water while mixing, until the flour reaches a damp, sandy consistency. This step is optional; you can use dry flour directly;
  2. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick skillet or frying pan over medium heat;
  3. Form the Crepes: Sprinkle a thin, even layer of tapioca flour onto the heated pan, about 1/4 cup per crepe. Use a spatula to spread it evenly into a circular shape, about 6-8 inches in diameter. The flour should stick together;
  4. Cook: Cook for about 1-2 minutes, until the edges start to lift and the center looks set. There’s no need to flip; the crepe cooks on one side;
  5. Add Fillings (optional): If desired, add your choice of fillings to one half of the crepe. Fold the other half over to cover the fillings;
  6. Finish Cooking: Cook for an additional minute until the cheese melts (if using) and the crepe is warm throughout;
  7. Serve: Remove from the pan and serve immediately. Enjoy your tapioca crepes hot, with your favorite fillings!

Feel free to experiment with different flavors and fillings!
